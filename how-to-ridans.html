How Ridan Composters Work

Ridan composters are innovative barrel-shaped systems designed for efficiently processing large volumes of food waste, making them particularly suitable for schools, businesses, and community gardens.

Key Features

  • Rotating drum design with hand crank
  • Fully enclosed system
  • Large capacity (typically 300-400 liters)
  • Can achieve hot composting temperatures

Operation Process

  1. Add balanced mix of food waste and woodchips
  2. Turn regularly using the handle
  3. Maintain proper moisture levels
  4. Harvest finished compost in 6-8 weeks

For optimal results, allow finished compost to mature in a ground-based bin for 1-2 months before use to enable soil organisms to complete the decomposition process.

Benefits of Ridan Systems

  • Rodent-proof - Fully enclosed design prevents pest access
  • Large capacity - Handles significant volumes of food waste
  • Efficient aeration - Easy turning promotes oxygen flow
  • Hot composting - Can reach temperatures that kill pathogens
  • Ideal for institutions - Perfect for schools and businesses

Considerations

  • Cost - Higher initial investment than simple bins
  • Space requirements - Needs significant installation area
  • Physical effort - Can be heavy to turn when full
  • Material balance - Requires careful mix of food waste and woodchips

Optimal Usage Tips

Material Preparation

Chop food waste into small pieces (2-3cm) and mix thoroughly with woodchips (approximately 1:1 ratio by volume) for best results.

Turning Frequency

Rotate the drum 5-10 times every 1-2 days. More frequent turning accelerates decomposition but requires more effort.

Moisture Control

Maintain a damp sponge consistency. Add water if dry, or more woodchips if too wet. Proper moisture prevents odor and speeds decomposition.
