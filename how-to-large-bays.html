Large Compost Bays

Large compost bays are ideal for hot-composting as they allow you to process large volumes of material quickly. You can construct them affordably using repurposed pallets or wood with wire mesh.

How Compost Bays Work

After construction, fill your bay with alternating layers of green and brown materials:

Fast Hot Composting

  • Fill completely within days
  • Temperatures reach 65Â°C+
  • Kills weeds and pathogens
  • Produces compost in weeks

Slow Cold Composting

  • Add materials gradually
  • Minimal temperature rise
  • Takes several months
  • Better for small quantities

Many gardeners use multiple bays to allow for turning compost between chambers.

Advantages

  • Cost-effective - Build from recycled pallets
  • Scalable - Ideal for large gardens/allotments
  • Efficient - Large mass retains heat
  • Productive - Makes substantial compost quickly

Considerations

  • Requires significant space
  • Demanding physical work
  • Turning can be labor-intensive
  • Rodent protection needed

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Site Selection

    Choose a location that's accessible but not too close to living areas (to avoid fruit flies). Allow 30cm clearance from fences and consider partial shade.

  2. Pest Prevention

    Line the base and sides with 6mm wire mesh to deter rodents, especially important when composting food waste.

  3. Foundation Layer

    Create a 10cm base of coarse browns (twigs, woodchips) to promote airflow.

  4. Material Preparation

    Chop ingredients to 5cm pieces for faster decomposition (optional but recommended).

  5. Moisture Management

    Maintain damp sponge consistency. Add water if dry, or more browns if soggy. Cover with cardboard/tarp to retain moisture.

  6. Layering Process

    Continue adding balanced layers until full. This may take days to months depending on your material volume.

  7. Monitoring

    Regularly check temperature, moisture, and decomposition progress.

  8. Harvesting

    When material is dark, crumbly, and unrecognizable, remove any undecomposed pieces for the next batch and use your finished compost.
